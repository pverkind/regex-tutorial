8. More precise quantification

Sometimes, it's not enough to state that a character may be present "one or more" times; you might want to state exactly how many times it may be present. This is expressed in regular expressions using the curly braces {} . For example, if you are searching for words that are exactly 5 characters, you could use this regex: \w{5}.

You may also want to allow a range of numbers: \w{5,8} will match any sequence of 5, 6, 7 or 8 word characters.

You can also leave out either the start or end number to express "maximum X" or "minimum X": \w{,8} will match any sequence of up to 8 word characters, while \w{8,} will match any sequence of minimum 8 word characters.
